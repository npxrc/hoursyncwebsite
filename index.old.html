HourSync is a modern way for students to submit their eHours.

The website the Olathe School District provides is clumsy, ugly, and looks depressing.

For starters, the default website sends you to a landing page with... nothing? Just a stretched image of your academy logo. And when you go to the eHours page, the submission button is completely disproportionate compared to the size of the other buttons on the page.

Next, the progress bar is massive, taking up valuable screen real estate. And the header and submission button makes it so you can't see the whole progress bar.

Lastly, their website does not work well with accesibility themes for those with impaired vision

These are just a few of the issues that the eHours portal has. However, there hasn't been a solution.

Until now. Introducing HourSync, an app designed using Microsoft's C# language to ensure smoothness and that it is efficient to run.

HourSync is a software that streamlines the process of viewing or submitting eHour requests, or even just seeing how many eHours you have.

It's simple, sleek, and was a lot of fun to create.
